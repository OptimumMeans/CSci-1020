The Sunny 16 Rule

Sunny 16 Rule
Aperature Lighting conditions Shadow Detail
f/22 Snow/sand Dark with sharp edges

The Sunny 16 rule is a photographic guideline that states that on a sunny day, when the ISO is set to 100 and the shutter speed is at its reciprocal value of the ISO (1/100th of a second), the aperture should be set to f/16. This will give a proper exposure for a scene that is lit by the sun. It is a simple and easy way to determine the correct exposure settings for outdoor photography without the need for a light meter. However, it is important to note that this rule is only a rough estimate and may not work in all lighting conditions. It is always a good idea to check the exposure with a light meter or by taking a test shot and adjusting as needed.

Creativity when composing

  1. Experiment with different perspectives, try shooting from a low angle or a high angle to add interest and change the feel of the image.
  2. Use the rule of thirds, imagine your frame divided into thirds both horizontally and vertically, place your subject on one of the intersections to add balance and tension to your composition.
  3. Incorporate leading lines, natural or man-made lines in your composition, they can lead the viewer's eye into the scene and help to convey a sense of depth.

Photography is a versatile medium that is used in a wide range of applications in the real world. It is used in art, journalism, advertising, scientific research, and many other fields. In art, photographers use their skills to create visually compelling images that can be used for fine art, commercial art, or social commentary. In journalism, photographers capture images that tell the story of important events and issues around the world. In advertising, photographers use their skills to create eye-catching images that help to sell products and services. Photography also plays a vital role in scientific research, as it is used to document and study everything from the tiniest microorganisms to the largest galaxies. Additionally, it is commonly used in the field of documentation and archiving, such as in the field of archaeology and architectural documentation. Photography is an essential tool for capturing and preserving the world around us, and its uses are continually expanding as technology advances.
