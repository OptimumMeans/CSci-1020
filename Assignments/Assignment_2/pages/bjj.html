Headings!

Headings and sub-headings, as well as sub-sub-headings are very important in the HTML hierarchy. They enable many 'Accessability' functions that a user may need, or they can be found at the footer of a site to save countless link-clicking and scrolling. Headings run 1-6, with 1 being the biggest (refer to "Camerons First Blog"). On this page I have only used h1 and h2, h6 is hilariously small. Never use more than 1 h1 tag! Ever!

Paragraph tags

The <p> tag is the basis of this blog. It enables written words rather than each word being tried as a command/element/tag. The tag is especially useful when equipped with an <article> tag, the two go hand-in-hand. It enables blocks of text that can be separated from other blocks of text, such as this blog and its multiple posts. I think the <p> tag is my favorite. It has enabled me to convey this to you.

Tags and Elements

A tag can be used to open or close an element. An opening tag consists of < and >, While a closing tag goes like this: </ and >. An element consists of a command that lets the computer understand what you are trying to display, whether it be a paragraph, or a sub-heading. Fun fact: I had to learn how to use the greater than and less than sign in HTML as those characters are specifically reserved for HTML.

Semantics

There are non-semantic and semantic elements. Non-semantic tells you absolutely nothing about the content enclosed. Semantic on the other hand defines the content and works for tags such as <article>, <form>, and <table>. Semantic elements are of high importance to Search Engines and the users devices who are perusing your website. It also has greater accessability for users who may be blind or have other impairments that require such levels of accessability.
